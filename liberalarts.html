Liberal Arts Education







The main difference between professional education and liberal arts is that the former offers more skills-based programs aimed at equipping a student with the skills and requirements to do a particular job, while the latterâ€™s mission is to impart general knowledge and skills. In other words, vocational education prepares students to work in narrowly focused areas with a set of specific requirements. A liberal education, by contrast, stimulates students to advance not only within the framework of their selected major, but also develop soft skills that are highly demanded in our century. For example, liberal arts programs traditionally include three main areas: science, arts and humanities, with the freedom to choose various courses to find a distinctive self. Technical education, on the other hand, consists of just specialized training with predetermined courses and topics. In the era of technological advancement, the demand in specialized education has increased. Consequently, the job market becomes more competitive and students are more likely to opt for career-oriented programs (generally to increase hiring chances and get high-paying employment). However, the core reason why more people choose vocational education is confusion and negative thinking about liberal arts. Many have the prejudice that liberal arts is just expensive luxury and counterproductive, since it places more emphasis on general knowledge that in their perspective is irrelevant in workspaces. Rather, it helps students to acquire soft skills, which are crucial in creating a successful career.
